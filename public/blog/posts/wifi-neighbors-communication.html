How I Communicated with My Neighbors Using Only WiFi Names

February 28, 2025 WiFi Humorist Humor

It all started innocently enough. I moved into a new apartment building and, while setting up my WiFi network, I noticed my neighbors had some pretty boring network names: "Smith Family WiFi," "Apartment 3B," and the ever-creative "Netgear123."

As someone who appreciates a good laugh, I couldn't resist naming my network something more entertaining. After browsing through some funny WiFi names, I settled on "FBI Surveillance Van #7." Classic, I know, but I thought it might get a chuckle from anyone who happened to be scanning for networks.

The First Response

About a week later, I was connecting a new device when I noticed something interesting. One of my neighbors had changed their network name to "NSA Listening Post #2." I laughed out loud. Someone had a sense of humor!

This was the beginning of what would become a six-month "conversation" conducted entirely through WiFi network names.

The Escalation

I decided to up the ante. I changed my network name to "CIA Drone Control Center." Two days later, a new network appeared: "Russian Hackers Inc."

The game was on. Over the next few months, our WiFi names evolved into an increasingly absurd dialogue:

Pretty Fly for a WiFi
Bill Wi the Science Fi
Wu-Tang LAN
The LAN Before Time
Silence of the LANs

The Plot Thickens

By month three, we had at least five participants in our little WiFi name game. The building's network list had become a showcase of creativity:

  • Hide Your Kids, Hide Your WiFi
  • It Burns When IP
  • The Password is Password
  • Nacho WiFi
  • Lord of the Pings

I was particularly proud when I changed mine to "Routers of Rohan" and someone responded with "One Does Not Simply Log In."

The Grand Finale

The climax of our WiFi name saga came during a building-wide power outage. When the electricity finally returned and networks started popping back up, I noticed a new one: "Building Meeting Saturday 2PM Lobby."

Curious, I actually attended this meeting, where I discovered that our property manager had organized a get-together specifically for the "WiFi name people" to meet in person. It turned out that our little network name game had become somewhat famous in the building, with residents eagerly checking for new names whenever they connected to their networks.

That Saturday, I met the faces behind "Russian Hackers Inc." (a retired English teacher), "Lord of the Pings" (a college student), and several others. We all brought snacks, shared laughs, and even set up a building-wide game night tradition that continues to this day.

Lessons Learned

What started as a simple joke turned into a community-building experience. Through our WiFi names, we created connections that extended beyond the digital realm and into real-life friendships.

So the next time you're setting up your network, remember that your WiFi name might just be the beginning of an unexpected adventure. Who knows? Your "Pretty Fly for a WiFi" might lead to meeting some pretty fly neighbors.
